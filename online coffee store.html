Our Products

Espresso:

A concentrated shot of coffee brewed by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee beans. It serves as the foundation for many other coffee drinks.

Cappuccino:

A classic Italian coffee beverage consisting of equal parts of espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam, usually topped with a sprinkle of cocoa or cinnamon.A cappuccino is the perfect balance of espresso, steamed milk and foam. This coffee is all about the structure and the even splitting of all elements into equal thirds. An expertly made cappuccino should be rich, but not acidic and have a mildly sweet flavouring from the milk.

Latte:

A popular coffee drink made with espresso and steamed milk, often topped with a small amount of milk foam. It's milder than a cappuccino and offers a creamy texture.

Americano:

Made by diluting a shot of espresso with hot water, resulting in a coffee that resembles a drip-brewed coffee but with the intensity of espresso.An americano is just water and espresso. It'll either be served 1/2 and 1/2 or 1/3 espresso to 2/3 water, depending on the coffee shop in question or how you've chosen to brew it. Traditionally, there will be no milk but some people may add it depending on their personal preference.

Macchiato:

An espresso "stained" or "marked" with a small amount of milk or milk foam. It's available in variations like caramel or vanilla macchiato.

Mocha:

A delightful blend of espresso, steamed milk, and chocolate syrup. It's often topped with whipped cream and chocolate drizzle.

Flat White:

Originating from Australia or New Zealand, it's made with espresso and microfoam (steamed milk with fine, velvety bubbles), resulting in a smooth and velvety coffee.A flat white is a blend of micro-foamed milk poured over a single or double shot of espresso. This microfoam is steamed milk infused with air, to create a smooth and velvety texture and creamy taste. Precise pouring and steaming of the microfoam is essential in making a flat white.

Turkish Coffee:

A traditional method of brewing coffee where very finely ground coffee is simmered with water and sometimes sugar in a special pot called a cezve or ibrik.

Cold Brew:

Coffee brewed with cold water over an extended period (usually 12-24 hours). It's known for its smooth, less acidic flavor and is often served over ice.
